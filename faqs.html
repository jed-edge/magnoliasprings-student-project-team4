Frequently Asked Questions

General

The festival takes place in historic downtown Magnolia Springs, Georgia, centering around the historic courthouse square. Public parking is available at the Magnolia Springs High School lot and the Community Center, both just a short walk from the square.

Yes! Designated ADA-accessible parking is located in the municipal lot directly behind the City Hall. From there, continuous, free golf cart shuttles are available to drop off guests at the main entrance gates on the square.

While we love our furry friends, pets are not permitted on the crowded courthouse lawn or inside the designated food courts for safety and sanitation reasons. Leashed service animals, of course, are always welcome.

We celebrate rain or shine! Grab your umbrella or poncho and join us anyway. In the event of severe weather (lightning, high winds), outdoor activities may be temporarily paused and resumed once the weather clears. Up-to-the-minute updates will be posted to our social media channels.

Yes, public restrooms are located inside the lower level of the Historic Courthouse and the Magnolia Springs Public Library. Additionally, portable restroom stations (including ADA-compliant units and a dedicated, quiet baby-care tent) are available on both the east and west ends of the festival grounds.

Money & Ticket

Admission to the Autumn Jubilee is completely free! There is no entry fee to access the festival grounds, view the live music performances, or stroll through the arts and crafts market.

Nearly all of our craft vendors and food trucks accept major credit/debit cards and mobile payments (Apple Pay, Google Pay). However, some of our smaller, traditional heritage craft vendors or kids' game booths may prefer cash, so carrying a little green is always a great idea!

For your convenience, temporary ATMs are located near the Main Information Booth at the corner of Spring Street and Broad Street, as well as inside the lobby of the Magnolia Springs Community Bank right on the square.

Yes, while general festival admission is free, a few special attractions require tickets. This includes the Saturday Night Heritage Concert ($15 at the gate or online) and the Taste of the Springs Food Tent ($10 for a tasting pass of 5 local dishes).

Ride and game tickets purchased at our festival ticket booths are non-refundable. However, they remain valid for both days of the festival weekend, so you can save any leftover tickets for day two!

Activities

Music kicks off on Friday at 4:00 PM and runs throughout the weekend! The full lineup of local bluegrass, folk, and Southern country artists—along with set times—can be found on our interactive "Schedule" page on the website.

Absolutely! The "Little Sprouts Kids Zone" is packed with family fun, including a pumpkin painting patch, a petting zoo, face painting, carnival rides, and free puppet shows scheduled throughout both days.

We love celebrating local talent! Entry forms for our famous Pecan Pie Bake-Off, Apple Butter contest, and the Giant Pumpkin competition are available on our website. Registrations must be submitted by the Friday morning before the festival begins.

Yes! We highly recommend bringing your favorite lawn chairs or picnic blankets to set up on the courthouse lawn. We just ask that you set up in the designated "seating zones" so we can keep the walkways clear for pedestrians.

You can download and print a PDF map directly from our website's "Plan Your Visit" page which will be available closer to the event. Physical festival brochures containing maps and schedules will also be handed out at all information booths and entrance gates.

Vendors

All prospective vendors can apply online through our website's "Vendor Portal." Simply fill out the application form, upload 3–5 high-quality photos of your handmade crafts or menu items, and submit your application fee.

Applications close on August 31st. Our jury panel reviews submissions to ensure a diverse and unique selection of crafts and food. You will receive an email notification regarding your acceptance status by September 15th.

Standard booth spaces do not include utilities. However, a limited number of spaces with 110v/20amp electrical hookups are available for an additional fee on a first-come, first-served basis. While there is public Wi-Fi around the courthouse square, we highly recommend that vendors have an independent cellular data plan or hotspot for processing transactions.

Yes! Professional, overnight security patrols the festival grounds from Friday at 8:00 PM until Saturday morning at 7:00 AM. While the area is secure, we advise vendors to take valuable merchandise, electronics, and cash boxes with them overnight.

The Magnolia Springs Autumn Jubilee is a rain-or-shine event, and booth fees are non-refundable. In the extreme case of dangerous, severe weather that forces a full festival cancellation, a partial credit toward next year's booth fee may be issued at the discretion of the festival committee.
